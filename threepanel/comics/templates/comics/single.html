Have you read this book?

Peopleware: Productive Products & Teams is a modern project-management classic, up there with The Mythical Man-Month.

The premise? Most of your software problems are not so much software problems as they are people problems.

It is about helping people be productive and getting problems out of their way so that they can kick ass.

What sort of problems? Things that kill productivity and passion, like overtime, noise, shooting for a poor-quality product, that sort of thing.

Android, Where?

I've been going on about wearables a lot, lately.

As much as I rip on the flashy new watches that don't do much except put notifications on your wrist, vibrate periodically, run out of battery life in one day, and offer all of the rest of the functionality of a Casio watch circa 1988, I totally bought an LG Urbane and I would totally recommend it to anybody insane enough to want to try out an Android Wear device.

I mean, I get tweets on my wrist. How cool is that?

Plus, it's classy as heck.
